Differentiation (Derivative) vs Gradient

Derivative is an fundamental and an essential tool in mathematics.

Gradient Descent method is an optimization method that is predominantly used especially in machine learning.

I was getting the conceptual idea of the GDM, but I was confusing why the derivative of a function gives us the slope of the tangent line and why the graident of a function gives us a vector that is perpendicular to the tangent vector, and how the vector gives direction of the function. Eventually, why we subtract the gradient in the gradient descent.

The full understanding of gradient was also needed to understand the optimization, since I am taking convex optimization course now.

Let’s dig into the gradient world!

  1. Derivative vs Gradient
Derivative

By definition in Wikipedia,

Derivative Definition: “The derivative of a function of a real variable measures the sensitivity to change of the function value (output value) with respect to a change in its argument (input value).”

Let $f$ be a real-valued function defined in an open neighborhood of a real number a.

The tangent line to a plane, a curve, or a graph at a given point, a, is the straight line that “just touches” the curve at that point. Technically, it doesn’t meet the graph as a parameter goes $\infty$.

Then, the derivative of $y$ with respect to x at a is the slope of the tangent line to the graph of $f$ at $(a,f(a))$.

Since the derivative is a measure of the change rate at which the value $y$ of the function changes with respect to the change of the $x$, we define the value $h$ close to zero to get a good approximation to the slope of the tangent line.

The slope of the tangent line will be defined as

$$\frac{f(a+h)-f(a)}{(a+h)-(a)} = \frac{f(a+h)-f(a)}{h}$$

We set the $h$ close to zero, and we make $h$ goes to $\infty$ if it exists,

$$f’(a) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(a+h)-f(a)}{h}$$

The $f’(a)$ is the derivative of the function $f$ at $a$.

Now, we know the rigorous definition of the tangent line and derivative.

Let’s see the tangent vector and gradient. 

Gradient

Gradient Definition: “The gradient is a multi-variable generalization of the derivative. Whereas the ordinary derivative of a function of a single variable is a scalar-valued function, the gradient of a function of several variables is a vector-valued function.”

It is also described as,

“Much as the derivative of a function of a single variable represents the slope of the tangent to the graph of the function, if at a point $mathcal{P}$, the gradient of a function of several variables is not the zero vector, it has the direction of greatest increase of the function at $\mathcal{P}$, and its magnitude is the rate of increase in that direction.”

By the definition, the difference between derivative and gradient is simply depends on the type of function, whether the function is scalar-valued or vector-valued.
And, the derivative gives the sloep of the tangent to the graph at a given point, and the gradient gives the direction of the function at a given point.
